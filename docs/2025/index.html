Event Motivation

With the rapid advancements in artificial intelligence (AI), particularly driven by deep learning, AI is poised to become an integral part of our daily lives. However, to ensure the safe and reliable deployment of AI in society, it is imperative that we develop AI systems capable of explaining their decision-making processes. This interpretability is crucial for verifying system correctness, guaranteeing quality, and fostering trust.

The Knowledge Graph Reasoning Challenge for Social Issues, a continuation of our successful Knowledge Graph Reasoning Challenge series, aims to address this critical need by focusing on a particularly pressing societal concern: the safety of older adults. According to recent studies, a significant portion of accidents involving older individuals occur within their homes. By setting the challenge in a familiar and relatable context, we hope to attract a broader audience and stimulate innovative solutions.

The challenge will provide a standardized dataset of knowledge graphs and videos, serving as a valuable resource for researchers and developers working on safety technologies for older adults and beyond. These technologies can potentially be integrated into various systems, such as cyber-physical systems, AI speakers, and AR systems, to detect accident risks, provide concrete explanations, and suggest safer alternatives. Such advancements can benefit not only older adults but also other vulnerable populations, including children, factory workers, and pedestrians.

By promoting the development of interpretable AI for social issues, we aim to contribute to a safer and more equitable future for all.
